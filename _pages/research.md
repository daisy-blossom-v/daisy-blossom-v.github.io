## Research Statement

My work focuses on the quantitative study of how AI adoption reshapes workforce systems — combining domain expertise in HR operations with causal inference methods. I am particularly interested in settings where organizational data can be used to estimate the causal impact of technology on worker behavior and well-being.

---

## Current Project

### AI Tool Adoption and Overtime Patterns Among IT Workers

**Motivation**: While managing HR operations at a ~1,400-person IT firm, I observed a 13% reduction in overtime hours following a targeted reduction campaign coinciding with generative AI adoption. This raised the question: how much of this shift is attributable to AI tool usage, and what organizational conditions shape that relationship?

**Data**: Stack Overflow Developer Survey (2023–2024) · publicly available · ~90,000 respondents

**Methods**: OLS regression, Difference-in-Differences (DiD)

**Research Questions**:
1. Does active use of AI productivity tools predict lower reported overtime and burnout among IT workers?
2. Is this effect moderated by peer-learning culture versus formal top-down training?
3. What organizational conditions amplify or dampen the labor productivity effects of AI adoption?

**Target Venue**: HFES Annual Meeting 2026

**Status**: Data analysis in progress
